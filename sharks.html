The Shark Facts

This is the area where you can learn facts about sharks in our world. If you look at images of sharks, you might see a ferocious monster with rows of sharp, scary teeth ready to kill anything it sees. But in reality, sharks are actually passive when near humans. Sharks are not the beasts described in Hollywood and in books, and swimming with sharks is moderately safe. However, there are some sharks that you should look out for. Tiger Sharks, Bull Sharks, and Great White Sharks can be a threat to humans. If you are wearing the right clothes though, you are lowering the risk of being bitten. Sharks will take a test bite on humans if the human looks like one of their prey. So if you don't look like one of the shark's common prey, you are very safe.

Great White Shark is the most well-known species of sharks in the world. It will be found on any coast of any major ocean. This species of sharks is of the three dangerous sharks to humans. The Great White Shark often hunts seal and other big fish. It goes underneath the seal, and uses its tail as a propellor to burst of out the water with the seal in its mouth. And in seconds, the shark has its meal.

The Tiger Shark can be found in temporate and tropical locations of the world, excluding the Medditeranean Sea. It is another one of the 3 sharks that can be a danger to humans. The Tiger Shark usually nocturnally hunts their prey. It is well known as a "garbage eater". Tiger Sharks that have been hunted or stranded on shores can carry a lot of weird items in their stomach. Many human items have been discovered in the stomach of a Tiger Shark. Items like shoes, hats, a barbie doll, and much much more.
